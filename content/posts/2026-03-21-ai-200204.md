The recent Cornell Health AI Hackathon showcased the transformative potential of artificial intelligence in healthcare. Over 100 students from various universities collaborated to develop innovative solutions aimed at addressing pressing health challenges, such as delayed diagnoses and inefficient clinical workflows. One standout project, SpineSight XR, utilized extended-reality imaging and AI to enhance scoliosis screening, demonstrating how technology can lead to earlier detection and better patient outcomes.

With the rapid advancements in AI tools, students are now able to prototype functional technologies in a fraction of the time it once took. This shift allows them to focus on solving real-world problems rather than getting bogged down in lengthy coding processes. As we look to the future, the question remains: how will these emerging technologies continue to reshape the landscape of healthcare delivery and patient care?
