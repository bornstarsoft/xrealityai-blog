As we stand on the brink of a technological revolution, Ericsson is positioning itself as a pivotal player in the development of AI-native networks that will serve as the foundation for 6G deployment. The company emphasizes that the transition from 5G to 6G will not just be an upgrade but a complete transformation, driven by intelligent systems that enhance performance and efficiency across all network layers.

Ericsson's strategy involves deep collaboration with industry partners, including Intel and NVIDIA, to create an interoperable ecosystem that supports emerging technologies like generative AI and physical AI applications. This collaborative approach is already yielding results, as demonstrated at Mobile World Congress 2026, where Ericsson showcased its advancements in AI-driven radio access networks and the integration of AI capabilities across various network components.

Looking ahead, the groundwork laid by Ericsson and its partners is crucial for the anticipated commercial rollout of 6G around 2030. With the integration of AI into every aspect of network architecture, the future of mobile connectivity promises to be not only faster but also smarter.
