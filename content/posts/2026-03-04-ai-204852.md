As South Korea's telecom giants gear up for the 6G era, they are redefining the role of wireless base stations. No longer just simple radio transmission points, these stations are evolving into sophisticated hubs for artificial intelligence computing. This transformation into AI-powered radio access networks (AI-RAN) is a strategic move to secure technological leadership in the competitive landscape of next-generation mobile networks.

By integrating AI capabilities with their existing infrastructure, these companies aim to enhance network performance and reliability. This shift not only positions them at the forefront of technological innovation but also opens doors for global partnerships that could set industry standards for the future. As we look ahead, the question remains: how will these advancements shape our connectivity and digital experiences in the coming years?
