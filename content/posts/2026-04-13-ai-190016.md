Materiality is emerging as a pivotal aspect of immersive technology, particularly in the realm of Extended Reality (XR). As highlighted by Nokia, the true essence of immersion lies in making digital experiences feel tangible. While visual and auditory elements have seen significant advancements, the sense of touch remains largely unaddressed, creating a gap in our digital interactions.

In a world where technology has predominantly focused on sight and sound, it's time to shift our attention to the tactile experience. Bridging this sensory gap is essential for creating more authentic and engaging digital environments. Imagine a future where virtual interactions are not only seen and heard but also felt, enhancing our connection to the digital realm.

As we look ahead, the challenge will be to integrate materiality into our digital experiences. How can we harness technology to create a more immersive and tactile world? The answers may redefine how we interact with technology and each other in the years to come.
