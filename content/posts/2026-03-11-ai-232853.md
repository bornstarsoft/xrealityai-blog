In the rapidly evolving world of extended reality (XR), the conversation around metrics is shifting from vanity metrics to key performance indicators (KPIs) that truly matter. Companies are no longer just experimenting with XR; they are looking for solid evidence that these immersive technologies are delivering tangible business outcomes. Metrics such as downtime reduction, error rates, and time-to-competency are becoming essential for securing executive buy-in and funding.

Executives want to know how XR can help them reduce risks, compress time, and avoid costs. Traditional training metrics like completion rates and satisfaction scores may feel reassuring, but they often lack the financial impact that boards are looking for. Instead, focusing on metrics that demonstrate real changes in operational performancelike improved error rates during peak loads or faster onboarding timescan make a compelling case for XR investments.

As we move forward, the challenge will be to ensure that XR metrics not only survive scrutiny from CFOs but also drive meaningful change in the workplace. Are we ready to embrace this new paradigm and leverage XR for true business transformation?
