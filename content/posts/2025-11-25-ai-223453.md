The 2025 Qingdao Virtual Reality Innovation Conference has emerged as a pivotal event, showcasing the rapid advancements in virtual reality (VR) and artificial intelligence (AI) that are reshaping industries. Held from November 21 to 22 in Qingdao, China, this conference gathered innovators, industry leaders, and researchers to explore the integration of VR and AI, aiming to position Qingdao as a national hub for digital economy development.

One of the standout features of this years conference was the launch of the Qingdao Virtual Reality Innovation and Experience Center, which combines cutting-edge technologies such as augmented reality, digital twins, and holographic displays with cultural storytelling. This initiative not only highlights the technological advancements but also emphasizes the importance of cultural integration in driving innovation.

As the Laoshan district continues to experience rapid growth in the VR and AI sectors, the combined output is projected to exceed 60 billion yuan by 2025. This growth is set to propel the digital economy towards a total value of nearly 120 billion yuan. With ambitious plans to surpass the 100-billion-yuan mark and establish a robust industrial fund, Qingdao is clearly on a trajectory to become a leader in the global digital landscape.
